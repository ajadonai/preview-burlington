Decision 1
What becomes the primary CTA across the site?
This determines the main button in the navigation, hero sections, and page footers. The other option becomes the secondary (ghost button) CTA.

Path A: Profile Assessment ($75) is primary

Lower barrier to entry. Captures more leads. Consultation becomes the upsell after the 48-hour written response. Best for volume.

Recommended

Path B: Consultation ($150) stays primary

Higher barrier, fewer but more qualified leads. Profile Assessment appears as secondary CTA. Best for premium positioning.

Path C: Equal weight — both CTAs side by side

Let the prospect self-select. Two buttons everywhere. More choice, but may create decision paralysis.

Safe middle ground
Decision 2
Does the detailed Services page replace the current one?
The new Services mockup includes six deliverables, visual timeline, transparent pricing ($20K with market comparison), and named team leads. It's significantly more detailed than the current version.

Path A: Full replacement

New detailed page takes over /services entirely. Visitors see pricing, timeline, deliverables immediately. Signals confidence and transparency.

Recommended

Path B: Current page stays, detailed version becomes a sub-page

Current /services remains as a lighter overview. Detailed version lives at /services/detail or similar. Two-click depth to see pricing.

Decision 3
Knowledge Center and Newsletter — launch together or phase?
The Knowledge Center needs content to be useful. The Burlington Brief needs the Knowledge Center to have a content home. They're connected, but content takes time to produce.

Path A: Launch both together

Ship the Knowledge Center with 3–4 launch articles (regulatory update, field of endeavour guide, RFE response framework, dual petition explainer) and the newsletter signup simultaneously.

Recommended

Path B: Knowledge Center first, newsletter later

Launch the Knowledge Center page structure now. Add newsletter capture once there's a regular content cadence (4+ articles published). Avoids collecting emails with nothing to send.

Lower commitment
Decision 4
Approved Cases page — launch with representative examples or wait for real data?
The case study library is the single highest-conversion page identified in the competitive analysis. But publishing fabricated cases would damage credibility if discovered. The mockup uses representative but anonymised examples.

Path A: Launch now with representative anonymised cases

Use real engagement patterns (field of endeavour, criteria, timelines) from actual work, anonymised. Not fabricated — drawn from real experience, just not attributed to specific clients.

Recommended

Path B: Hold until clients consent to case publication

Wait until 4–6 clients agree to have their anonymised outcomes published. More defensible but delays the highest-impact page.

Decision 5
Assessment tools — deploy now or hold?
The V3 Benchmark and V4 Advisor are self-service eligibility quizzes for cold traffic. They live on a separate subdomain and funnel to the $75 Profile Assessment. They're a marketing tool, not a core site feature.

Path A: Deploy on assess.burlingtonconsult.com now

Start driving cold traffic (LinkedIn ads, email campaigns) to the free quiz immediately. Begin A/B testing V3 vs V4. Build the email list early.

Path B: Hold until main site pages are live

Get the six site pages approved and deployed first. Then launch assessment tools once the funnel endpoint ($75 Profile Assessment page) is actually live. Avoids dead-end links.

Recommended
Decision 6
Pathways page — gets a nav item or lives in the footer?
The Pathways page segments audiences by profession (STEM, Creative, Business, Medicine, Law, Academia). Adding it to the nav makes it highly visible but adds a fifth item. Keeping it in the footer preserves the clean four-item nav.

Path A: Add to primary navigation

Nav becomes: About · Services · Team · Cases · Pathways. Five items plus CTA. More discoverable but slightly heavier nav.

Path B: Footer and homepage only

Pathways lives in the footer column and is linked from the homepage hero or About page. Nav stays clean at four items. Accessed through contextual discovery.

Recommended
